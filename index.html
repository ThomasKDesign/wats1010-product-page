Save Time and Money!

Who has the time to write codes and functions to make an elaborate, interactive site? Maybe a few developers do. APIs essentially gives you all the foundation to stack all your codes on, so you concentrate on your content, and not the intricate programming behind it all! APIs are great time savers. They offer developers convenience in many cases.


What can our APIs do?

On the Web, our APIs make it possible for big services like Google Maps or Facebook to let other apps "piggyback" on their offerings. Think about the way Yelp, for instance, displays nearby restaurants on a Google Map in its app, or the way some video games now let players chat, post high scores and invite friends to play via Facebook, right there in the middle of a game.


What are APIs?


APIs make possible a sprawling array of Web-service "mashups," in which developers use mix and match APIs from the likes of Google or Facebook or Twitter to create entirely new apps and services. In many ways, the widespread availability of APIs for major services is what's made the modern Web experience possible.
